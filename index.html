Recent projects

Hootsuite Campaigns

Role: Software developer

Hootsuite Campaigns allows Hootsuite users to create their own online contest and galleries, such as a video competition or a simple instagram feed

Three of my favourite technologies in this project are Laravel, React, and the Jasmine Javascript unit testing framework

Film Hearts

Role: Back and front-end developer

Great films shouldn't be seen alone. The premise of Film Hearts is that people often end up going to films at festivals such as TIFF and VIFF by themselves, since the films are usually more alternative that normal and only appeal to a narrow market. Film Hearts attempts to connect like-minded movie goers, so that they can have a Movie Buddy / Fake Date to watch the movie with.

For this project I used the Laravel PHP framework which I found to be amazing.

Kiwibank Home Hunter

Role: Back-end developer

Home Hunter is an app for browsing property listings, but customers can also apply for a pre-approved home loan and get an indication of whether they can afford to buy each property. We needed to build not only the website but also the RESTful API that fuelled the website, iOS, and Android apps.

Klim

Role: Back and front-end developer

Klim is milk spelled backwards. It is also the name of one of the world's leading type foundries. Kris (the owner) wanted us to create a new way for his customers to try out his fonts. We came up with a simple and elegant tool where customers can create custom specimens using a mixture of fonts, and share them with friends.

NZ On Air: Irirangi Te Motu

Role: Back-end developer

Each page of this site has such a range of content on it, we needed to find a new way for the client to edit things. We found the answer in Sir Trevor. We've created all kinds of custom blocks that the site administrators can now use when editing each page in our CMS, I don't know how we ever did content management without it!

Kiwibank

Large corporate websites are always a challenge to try and keep simple. We've been working hard to make the majority of the site responsive and light weight (don't look at the personal banking section yet, it's next on the list!).
